# **Lead-Capture Business Plan**

## **Business concept**

Start a simple service business from home that helps local service companies capture more leads.

The offer:

“I install a simple lead-capture system for contractors and local service businesses so they stop losing quote requests when they miss calls, get busy, or customers contact them after hours.”

The first package should include:

* Google Business Profile cleanup  
* Simple 1-page website or landing page  
* Request-a-quote form  
* Missed-call / after-hours auto-reply setup  
* Review request templates  
* AI-written service copy and FAQs  
* Basic lead tracking sheet  
* Optional monthly maintenance

The business model:

* One-time setup fee  
* Monthly maintenance fee  
* Optional upsells later

Starter pricing:

* Intro setup: $499  
* Better setup: $799  
* Full setup: $999  
* Monthly maintenance: $99 to $149/month

# **Phase 1: Pick the niche and offer**

1. **1\. Choose one starting niche**

Do not start with “I help all businesses.”

Pick one type first.

Good first niches:

* Plumbers  
* HVAC companies  
* Roofers  
* Landscapers  
* Cleaners  
* Handymen  
* Junk removal companies  
* Auto detailers  
* Small remodelers

Best first choice:

Plumbers or HVAC companies.

Reason:

* One missed job can be worth hundreds or thousands.  
* They get urgent calls.  
* They often miss calls while working.  
* Quote forms, missed-call texts, reviews, and Google cleanup are easy to understand.

Checklist:

* Choose one niche.  
* Write down the niche.  
* Choose 3 nearby cities or regions to target.  
* Write down the top 5 services that niche usually sells.  
* Write down the main customer problem.  
* Write down the business-owner problem.

Example:

Niche: HVAC companies

Customer problem:

“My AC is broken and I need someone fast.”

Business-owner problem:

“We miss calls while on jobs and lose customers to the next company.”

Your offer:

“I help HVAC companies capture missed calls and quote requests with a simple website, form, text-back workflow, review system, and lead tracker.”

# **Phase 6: Build missed-call and after-hours templates**

- [ ] **23\. Create missed-call message templates**

These are copy/paste templates you can use in phone platforms.

General missed-call text:

“Sorry we missed your call — this is \[Business Name\]. What can we help with? Reply here or request a quote here: \[QUOTE\_FORM\_LINK\].”

After-hours text:

“Thanks for calling \[Business Name\]. We’re currently closed, but we can still get your request started. Reply with what you need or fill out this quick form: \[QUOTE\_FORM\_LINK\]. We’ll follow up when we reopen.”

Urgent-service text:

“Thanks for reaching out to \[Business Name\]. If this is urgent, reply with ‘urgent’ and a short description of what’s happening. You can also send photos here: \[QUOTE\_FORM\_LINK\].”

Photo request text:

“Can you send a few photos of the issue? That helps us understand the job before we call back.”

No-response follow-up:

“Just checking back — do you still need help with \[SERVICE\]? If so, reply here or use this form and we’ll follow up: \[QUOTE\_FORM\_LINK\].”

- [ ] **24\. Decide how to implement missed-call text-back**

There are 3 levels.

Level 1:

Give the client templates only.

Good for starter package.

Level 2:

Set them up on a business phone platform with auto-replies.

Good for $799+ package.

Level 3:

Custom Twilio/Telnyx automation.

Good later, not for the first few clients unless needed.

Recommended early approach:

Use a simple phone platform and make the client pay the software directly.

Checklist:

* Pick one phone platform to recommend.  
* Create setup instructions.  
* Create missed-call template.  
* Create after-hours template.  
* Test with your own number if possible.  
* Decide whether this is included or an add-on.  
* Add software costs to your proposal language.

Recommended client wording:

“Missed-call text-back requires a compatible phone/SMS platform. I can help set this up, but the software subscription is billed directly to your business.”
